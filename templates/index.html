The thyroid gland is a vital hormone gland: It plays a major role in the metabolism, growth and development of the human body. It helps to regulate many body functions by constantly releasing a steady amount of thyroid hormones into the bloodstream.
This organ is found at the front of the neck, under the voice box. It is butterfly-shaped: The two side lobes lie against and around the windpipe (trachea), and are connected at the front by a narrow strip of tissue.

Symptoms of hypothyroidism

  • Weight gain
  • Depression
  • Fatigue
  • Hair loss
  • Memory loss
  • Dry, rough skin
  • Muscle cramps

Symptoms of hyperthyroidism

  • Restlessness
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Anxiety
  • Weakness
  • Weight loss
  • Sweating
  • Nervousness
